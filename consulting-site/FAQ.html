Online therapy is carried out over the internet, primarily via live video call; It is the name given to the professional mental health counseling service that brings the client and therapist together in a virtual environment. This type of therapy is also called e-therapy, teletherapy, telemental health and online counseling. Online therapy, which has become rapidly popular thanks to the isolation caused by the COVID-19 pandemic process and continuing life remotely, is gradually decreasing as the difficult conditions required by the pandemic; It continues to maintain and even increase its popularity. Unlike therapy performed traditionally, that is, face to face, online therapy; It provides the person with the opportunity to participate in therapy with his/her therapist from any environment at times that are convenient for both of them. As long as the person meets the maximum conditions that will not disrupt the flow of therapy, it does not pose any problem for the person to participate in therapy from wherever he or she wants. In this sense, online therapy provides flexibility to both parties beyond spatial and even temporal boundaries.

Psychotherapy is a treatment approach based on a certain theory or paradigm, carried out by expert psychologists, in order to understand the person's feelings, thoughts and behaviors and to combat the difficulties he experiences regarding these. Psychotherapy can be carried out using different methods and techniques according to the needs of the person.


Psychologists are experts who study human behavior, emotions and thoughts and have graduated from the Psychology undergraduate department of universities. Psychologists can gain expertise with a master's degree and gain the authority to become a psychotherapist with therapy training. Psychiatrists, on the other hand, are people who have completed their undergraduate degree in medical school and specialize in mental health. Psychiatrists can evaluate people's mental health, recommend medication, and gain the authority to become a psychotherapist by completing the necessary therapy training.
